## Ours improvement summary (new tta_ctx_local)

**What changed in the new Ours.** The previous A2TTA(-Lite) column used the legacy `a2tta_lite` variant, which does *selective* online TTA: at each step it adapts only a budgeted subset (~25%) of nodes, drawn from a candidate pool and ranked by an error / uncertainty / distribution-shift / recency score. The new Ours (`tta_ctx_local`) removes the node-selection machinery entirely and instead combines **(i) a single global calibrator updated on ALL delayed labels** (stable, selection-free) with **(ii) a discardable per-batch local clone** that is briefly specialised to the current batch's context (relevance-weighted) and discarded afterwards. The frozen backbone (TrafficStream / Online-AN) and the FiLM residual calibrator are unchanged; only the online-adaptation rule differs. This "stable-global + ephemeral-local" design is simpler and uniformly stronger — 9/9 PEMS improve on every metric (Table A), with a mean MAE drop of −4.1% vs. the old Ours and −7.7% vs. the frozen backbone (Table B). On TFNSW it likewise gives the best non-foundation-model result (Avg MAE 99.75).
